Japantown Immersive

For One Night Only, Japantown's Past and Future Will Come Alive with Music, Dance, and Interactive Experiences


We've created big experiences before. 14-acre fictional cities. 100 actors. You get the gist.

Now, Epic and San Jose Taiko are teaming up on an experience the size of San Jose's Japantown. A whole neighborhood will be transformed as four city blocks become the backdrop for an experiential journey into historic Japantown's origins and future.

Japantown Immersive will see the return of Swingposium, Epic's breathtaking collaboration with San Jose Taiko. This taiko/swing-dancing celebration takes guests into the swing dance events that blossomed amidst the hardships of the Japanese-American internment camps. Swingposium featured a first-of-its-kind blending of a 30-piece brass band, taiko drums, swing dance, and immersive theatre. Video of Swingposium has been viewed 1.8 million times on Facebook. Now, you'll get the chance to experience it live!

Get ready to rock out to the tunes of taiko drums, big band music, and even electronic jams and hip hop as Japantown's past all comes alive at once. Historic storefronts will be transformed into their past selves. You'll venture through Japantown pounding mochi, playing old-school Japanese card games, trying on traditional outfits, encountering environments and people from the past, and dancing to San Jose Taiko's remarkable fusion concert with The Bangerz.

Oh, and one more thing! Japantown Immersive is a free event, thanks to generous grants by the Knight Foundation & California Arts Council Creative California Communities. So, mark your calendars for May 12 from 5:30pm to 9:00pm, and join us in San Jose's Japantown.

Make sure you join the Japantown Immersive Alert List for special updates, parking info, and tips on how best to experience Japantown Immersive (hint: definitely make reservations at one of Japantown's sushi joints)!

Japantown Immersive

The evening kicks off at 5:30PM at the San Jose Buddhist Church, where San Jose Taiko started 45 years ago. From there, attendees can enjoy three types of experiences:


HANDS-ON EXPERIENCES

Learn to play Hanafuda (a classic Japanese card game)

LED uchiwa (fan) making

Learn about the Internment Camp experience

Bon Odori pop-up dance school

Japantown Secrets Scavenger Hunt

Learn the Japanese New Year's tradition of pounding mochi

POP-UP PERFORMANCES

Brief 10-minute Street Performances from Japantown-Based Artists

San Jose Betsuin Youth & Adult Choir

Kyle Abbott and Bachido (shamisen)

Containher (vocalist)

Steve Nakano (jazz)

Halau Na Wai Ola (hula)

Lotus Preschool Taiko

Asagao Taiko (Yu-Ai Kai senior group)

MARQUEE PERFORMANCES

Large-Scale Collaborative Experiences

Swingposium (40 minutes): Epic Immersive, Wesley Jazz Ensemble, and San Jose Taiko join forces once again to celebrate how Big Band music & dance were sources of hope at WWII Internment Camps. If you missed last year's time-travel experience, join us as we bring you a condensed version.

SJT/DJ Cutso/The Get Down Studio (40 minutes): For those of you who enjoyed our collaboration with The Bangerz, we're bringing it back to the streets! Japantown residents DJ Cutso provides the tracks, The Get Down Dance Studios provide the moves, and we provide the taiko.
