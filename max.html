Biography

Bora "Max" Koknar

Bora "Max" Koknar is the Associate Artistic Director for Epic Immersive Labs.

As a leading man, Max has also originated many of Epic's iconic roles, from Matthew Briar in Matthew Briar and the Age of Resurrection to Dante Alighieri in The Infernal Motel.

As a producer, Max was instrumental in the launch of Epic's 100-actor, 14-acre epic Matthew Briar and the Age of Resurrection, Epic's expansion to Fort Mason in the sprawling hip-hop collaboration Ancien Regime, and Epic's hit San Francisco experience The Infernal Motel, which sold out its entire six-month run. He was a key player in Epic's pivot to developing corporate experiences, including a custom VR-integrated commission for Google at the historic Fallon House called The Syndicate, a large-scale custom commission for Google at The Mountain Winery called The Queen on The Mountain, an Alice in Wonderland-based underground commission for Facebook called Wonderland, and Apple's 1800-person holiday party at The San Francisco Museum of Modern Art, called Midnight at the Museum. Max was the co-creator of Swingposium, a partnership with San Jose Taiko and The Knight Foundation that used immersive techniques to bring to life the story of the swing dance events at the Japanese-American internment camps and even featured survivor Yone Fukui playing live in the band.

Max heads up Epic Immersive's Authentic Story Initiative, a program to radically expand diversity in immersive theatre by ensuring that all of Epic's stories feature at least 50% artists of color and 50% women. That commitment has shone through all of Epic's 100-actor epics, long-running shows, corporate experiences, and story selection, as Epic has collaborated with multidisciplinary and multicultural groups like taiko drummers, ballet folklorico dancers, hip-hop artists, classical Indian musicians, butoh artists, and more.

This year, Max served as the right-hand man to Lead Producer Steve M. Boyle in the creation of the first Immersive Design Summit, a gathering of the immersive universe at The Speakeasy in San Francisco featuring Diana Williams (Cofounder of ILMxLab and member of Lucasfilm's famed Story Group, which keeps the Star Wars canon), Colin Nightingale (Creative Producer for Punchdrunk International and part of the team that brought Sleep No More to New York and Shanghai), Sara Thacher (Creative Lead for Walt Disney Imagineering Research & Development), Vince Kadlubek (CEO of Meow Wolf), Yelena Rachitsky (Executive Producer for Oculus), Curtis Hickman (Cofounder of The Void), Brent Bushnell (CEO of Two Bit Circus), and more. The Summit rallied a global community, with guests from as far as New Zealand, Australia, France, England, The Netherlands, Mexico, and Colombia.
